Your Experience
Who this training is for.
Before You Apply

Anyone called to this work

No previous experience required. If you feel called to walk alongside families through pregnancy, birth, and the early weeks of parenthood, you belong here.

  • People with no birth-work experience whatsoever
  • Anyone with lived experience of pregnancy or maternal health
  • Community members who want to give back to Louisiana mothers
  • Peer supporters, advocates, and friends-of-friends already doing the work informally
  • Nurses, midwives, and other birth workers seeking ICEA credentials

Prerequisites

  • At least 18 years of age
  • Complete all pre-work modules before Night 1
  • Reliable internet, computer or tablet with camera and microphone
  • Full attendance at all four live sessions (10% absence cap)
What you'll learn.

Physiology of birth

Stages and phases of labor, hormones, Six P's, ACOG active-labor thresholds.

Comfort measures

Counter-pressure, hip squeezes, rebozo, birth ball work, acupressure, water therapy.

Client interviewing

Active listening, social determinants of health screening, BRAIN framework.

Newborn care

Diapering, paced bottle feeding, burping, rebath method, newborn massage.

Scope of practice

Where the doula's role begins and ends. How to stay in your lane, gracefully.

Postpartum mental health

Perinatal mood disorders, postpartum psychosis, trauma-informed escalation.

What's included.

Your tuition includes everything you need to start the work, mailed to you, online, and in your hands.

Logistics
Your path to ICEA Certified Birth Doula.
Certification Pathway

Training is step one. Here's the full arc from application to proctored exam, the MHN volunteer network supports you every step of the way, and active volunteers get exam prep free.

01

Complete coursework

Finish all pre-work, both virtual nights, both in-person days, and pass skills check-offs.

02

Join the network

Graduates are invited to join MHN's volunteer doula network and sign a Service Agreement.

03

Coating ceremony

MHN's welcoming into the volunteer community, part ceremony, part commissioning.

04

Get paired

Submit availability, match with a team member, and begin serving your first client.

05

Three births

Attend a minimum of three births, each at least six hours, and submit documentation.

06

Submit to ICEA

Package birth experience evidence through ICEA's traditional Birth Doula Pathway.

07

Free exam prep

Active MHN volunteers receive free access to our online exam prep class.

08

Take the exam

Sit for the proctored ICEA Certified Birth Doula exam. ICEA charges a separate exam fee, paid directly to ICEA at registration. Pass it and you are a credentialed doula.

You graduate into a team, not on your own.
After the Course

MHN doulas never work alone. Every client is supported by a two-doula team, coordinated by a staff doula, with ongoing education, hospital privileging support, and backup coverage when life happens. The training ends; the network doesn't.

  • Two-doula team pairingClients are supported by a pair, so there's always coverage and no one is ever on call alone.
  • One to two year service commitmentMinimum two client teams per year. Maximum six, we protect you from burnout.
  • Staff doula supportYour Volunteer Coordinator coordinates schedules, matches, escalations, and questions at any hour.
  • Free ICEA exam prepActive volunteers get free online exam prep ahead of their proctored certification exam.
  • Hospital privileging supportWe handle the paperwork, vaccinations, screening, badges, scrubs, HIPAA compliance.

Attendance and assessment policy.

Attendance & assessment policy.

Grading is Pass/Fail. To pass, complete all pre-work, attend every live session (10% absence cap), and demonstrate required skills on instructor sign-off. You get two skills check-off opportunities, first on Day 1 (comfort measures), final on Day 2 (all skills). If you don't pass by Day 2, you'll retake the full course.

Refund policy. Exceeding the attendance cap without excused absence requires a full course retake. Refunds are at administrator discretion for documented medical, family, or professional emergencies.
