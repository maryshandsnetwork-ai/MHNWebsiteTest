Welcome to the Program

You have stepped into something meaningful. This course is your foundation, the evidence base, the clinical awareness, the practical skills, and the professional grounding you need to walk alongside families on the most important day of their lives.

This page is your home base. Below, you will find everything you need to know about this course's structure, what to expect in each session, what to complete before we meet, and how to reach your instructor. Read it carefully and come back to it often.

“It is a holy and sacred role to be a support, a guide, a servant, and a doula. It is a gift and a treasure that we get to be in that space, and it is a gift and a treasure for that woman.”

— Madeline LeBlanc, RN, MHA, IBCLC, ICEA Trainer

How This Course Works

This is a hybrid program, meaning your learning unfolds across two different environments, online virtual sessions and in-person skills training. Both are required. Both are equally important. Here is what to expect from each.

Online Evening Sessions

Two nights  ·  5:30 PM – 8:00 PM CST  ·  30-minute break included

Our online nights cover the clinical and theoretical foundation of birth work: pregnancy physiology, labor stages, induction, medications, complications, and bereavement. You will engage in live discussions, breakout groups, and case studies, and complete short assignments before each in-person day.

In-Person Training Days

Two days  ·  8:30 AM – 4:30 PM  ·  Lunch and breaks included

Our in-person days are where theory becomes practice. Hands-on comfort measure stations, clinical skills labs, role play with real nurses, client interview practice, newborn care demonstrations, and your certification pathway review. Come rested, in comfortable clothes, and ready to move.

Session by Session Overview

Below is a detailed look at what will be covered in each of the four sessions. Review this carefully so you arrive prepared and know what to expect.

1

Pregnancy Physiology & When Pregnancy Goes Wrong

Online Night 1  ·  Virtual via Zoom  ·  5:30 PM – 8:00 PM CST

We begin at the beginning, the remarkable, complex process of pregnancy. We will trace physical and emotional changes across all three trimesters, explore the doula's role in prenatal care and client relationships, and then honestly examine what happens when pregnancy becomes complicated. This session builds your clinical fluency and your confidence to support clients through uncertainty.

—  Trimester-by-trimester pregnancy changes

—  OB danger signs: preeclampsia, abruption, PTL

—  Emotional and psychological shifts in pregnancy

—  HELLP syndrome, gestational diabetes, and HG

—  Prenatal doula visits and scope of practice

—  Doula language when complications arise

—  Supporting birth partners prenatally

—  Maternal mortality and health equity discussion

Assignment due before Night 2: Mission and Vision

2

Labor, Birth, Induction, Medications & Loss

Online Night 2  ·  Virtual via Zoom  ·  5:30 PM – 8:00 PM CST

Tonight we follow the baby from the very first contraction to the moment of birth, and we do not look away from the hard parts. We cover the stages of labor in clinical depth, explore induction and augmentation, demystify medications, walk through birth complications and cesarean birth, and close with a grounded, compassionate introduction to perinatal loss.

—  Stages and phases of labor (ACOG 2014 guidelines)

—  Epidurals, spinals, IV narcotics, doula scope

—  Labor hormones: oxytocin, endorphins, adrenaline

—  Peanut ball and bed-based positioning

—  Induction: medically indicated vs. elective (ARRIVE)

—  Dystocias, cesarean birth, and VBAC support

—  Augmentation: Pitocin, AROM, back labor

—  Perinatal loss: language, presence, and referral

 

3

Pain Management, Comfort Measures & Client Skills

In-Person Day 1  ·  MHN Training Center  ·  8:30 AM – 4:30 PM

Day 1 is experiential from start to finish. We open with the science of pain in labor, then we get on our feet. Wear comfortable clothing and closed-toe shoes. You will be demonstrating skills and moving through stations all day.

—  Gate Control and Neuromatrix pain theories

—  Doula, Nurse, and Client role play — four scenarios

—  The Three R's: Relaxation, Rhythm, Ritual

—  Initial client interview with MHN pre-birth packet

—  Six comfort measure stations with skills sign-off

—  Social Determinants of Health resource mapping

—  Rebozo, counter-pressure, birth ball, positioning

—  Birth plan creation and BRAIN framework practice

Resource Guide due at the start of In-Person Day 1

4

Postpartum, Newborn Care, Mental Health & Professionalism

In-Person Day 2  ·  MHN Training Center  ·  8:30 AM – 4:30 PM

Our final day turns toward what comes after, the fourth trimester and everything it holds. We cover postpartum physical recovery, breastfeeding basics, a full newborn skills lab, postpartum mental health including escalation protocols, your ICEA certification pathway, and the professional foundations every sustainable doula practice requires.

—  Postpartum physical changes and red flags

—  Rebirth and rebath bathing method — demo and practice

—  Breastfeeding basics, latch support, and IBCLC referral

—  Newborn massage sequence — teaching parents

—  Newborn skills lab: diapering and umbilical cord care

—  Postpartum mood disorders: screening and escalation

—  Paced bottle feeding and burping techniques

—  ICEA ICBD certification pathway and timeline planning

Before We Meet — Required Prework

All prework must be completed before the first night of online training. All six quizzes must be passed before you can access Night 1 content. Do not wait until the last minute, give yourself time to read carefully and absorb the material.

Important — Prework Requirements

All six prework quizzes must be passed before Night 1. The Night 2 module quiz must be passed before you may attend In-Person Day 1. Your Resource Guide must be submitted at the start of In-Person Day 1. Do not arrive without it. Students who have not completed the required prework will not be permitted to participate in live sessions. No exceptions will be made.

Your prework consists of three parts:

Part 1: MHN Field Guide

Read the full Field Guide (45–75 min), then complete the 47-question open-book quiz in Canvas. Covers MHN mission, scope of practice, client care processes, documentation, and team coordination.

80% passing score  ·  3 attempts  ·  Due before Night 1

Part 2: ICEA Position Papers

Read all five ICEA position papers (15–20 min each), then complete one short open-notes quiz per paper. Topics: Role and Scope, Family-Centered Care, Physiologic Birth, Comfort Measures, and Infant Feeding.

80% passing score each  ·  3 attempts each  ·  Due before Night 1

Part 3: Community Resource Guide

Research and compile 5 local resources across 10 categories (50 total) covering food assistance, mental health, housing, legal aid, healthcare, and more. See the Resource Guide assignment page in the Prework module for full instructions.

⚠  Due at the start of In-Person Day 1 — No late submissions accepted

See the Prework module in Canvas for all materials, quiz links, and the Resource Guide assignment page.

Certification Requirements

This course follows a Pass/Fail grading format. Completing the training is only the first step toward your ICEA ICBD credential. Below are all six requirements you must fulfill before you are eligible to sit for the certification exam.

1

Complete All Coursework

Pass all online module exams, submit all assignments, and meet minimum attendance requirements for both online and in-person sessions.

 2

Skills Sign-Off

All required comfort measures and newborn care skills must be demonstrated and signed off by your instructor during in-person training days.

 3

Enroll in ICEA Pathway

Submit your enrollment into the ICEA Birth Doula Traditional Pathway, download and review your certification materials, and exam study guide.
4

Birth Experiences

Attend a minimum of three birth experiences, each at least six hours in length. Submit all required birth experience documentation for each birth attended.

 5

ICEA Documentation

Submit all ICEA Birth Doula Pathway Program documents and your ICEA certification exam application through your instructor for processing.

 6

Certification Exam

Once all requirements are verified by your instructor, your exam application will be submitted to ICEA, and you will be eligible to sit for the ICBD exam.

Key Takeaways — What to Know Before Night 1

  • Complete all prework and pass all six quizzes (Quiz 1: MHN Field Guide and Quizzes 2–6: ICEA Position Papers) before the first night of training
  • Begin your Resource Guide early — it is due at the start of In-Person Day 1 and cannot be submitted late
  • Bring your MHN Field Guide and recommended texts to both in-person training days
  • You must pass the Night 2 quiz before attending In-Person Day 1 — plan your schedule accordingly
  • Wear comfortable, closed-toe shoes and clothes you can move in to both in-person days
  • Use the Modules tab in Canvas to navigate course content in order — modules are sequential and locked until the prior quiz is passed
  • You are not alone in this. Your cohort and your instructor are here every step of the way
