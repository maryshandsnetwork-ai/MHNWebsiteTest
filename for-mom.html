For Mothers-to-Be

A doula by your side, at no cost.

Mary's Hands Network provides free doula services to any mom who applies. Our services are limited by the availability and location of our volunteers, but every mother who applies is cared for to the fullest extent we are able.

Your doula team will be on call around your due date. They will follow up within 72 hours of delivery, with one to two visits between birth and 8 weeks postpartum. After your 6–8 week visit, our administrative team provides continued check-ins at 4, 6, and 12 months.

Ready to apply?

Complete your application online. The questionnaire covers your social connections, food and housing security, transportation, and general health.

  • Takes 15–20 minutes
  • Fully confidential (HIPAA)
  • You self-schedule onboarding

Your journey with us.

From match to 8 weeks postpartum, your doula team is your companion through every stage.

Stage 01

Application & Onboarding

Complete the application, sign agreements, and meet with a staff doula.

Stage 02

Team Match

Matched with two volunteer doulas within a week, introduced virtually.

Stage 03

Pre-Birth Visits

Two in-person visits to build your birth vision, practice comfort measures, and plan.

Stage 04

Labor, Birth & Postpartum

On-call support during your birth month, 72-hour check-in, up to 8 weeks of follow-up.

When the birth you prepared for is not the one you have.
Loss & Bereavement

Miscarriage, stillbirth, medical termination, or neonatal loss are a kind of grief the world rarely has good language for. MHN is a place where your loss is named, your baby is named, and you are not alone.

Our bereavement-trained doulas walk alongside families through early pregnancy loss, late loss, and the long, non-linear work that follows. We don't replace the hospital chaplain, the perinatal bereavement coordinator, or your own faith community, we accompany you between those visits, and we stay after everyone else has gone home.

"I'm so sorry. I'm here. That is what a doula says when there are no good words, and it is enough."

What we can support

Early pregnancy loss Stillbirth Termination for medical reasons Neonatal loss Traumatic birth processing Memory-making & rituals
