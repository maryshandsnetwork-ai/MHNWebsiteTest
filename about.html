About Mary's Hands Network

Named for Mary of the Visitation, the first doula in written history.

A Louisiana 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded in March 2023 to close the maternal health equity gap, one family, one birth, one pair of hands at a time.

Mission & Vision

What we believe. Where we are headed.

Our mission

To empower families, promote birth excellence, and bridge the health equity gap in Louisiana through trained volunteer community doulas, wrap-around services, and rigorous outcomes research.

Our vision

A Louisiana where every birthing person, regardless of income, background, or zip code, has free access to continuous, culturally humble doula support. Where every birth can be a space of dignity, connection, and power.

Our Outcomes · IRB-Approved Research

The case for doula care, made with real evidence.

Louisiana ranks among the worst U.S. states for maternal and infant health. Maternal mortality is 37.3 per 100,000 births, nearly twice the national rate. Black women face a mortality risk three to four times higher than white women. These are the outcomes MHN families are measured against.

63%
Cesarean Reduction

MHN clients: 13.3% C-section rate vs. Louisiana baseline of 36.1%. Statistically significant (p<0.001). The lowest rate among comparable community doula programs nationally.

83%
Preterm Birth Reduction

MHN clients: 2.2% preterm rate vs. Louisiana baseline of 13.4%. For Black women specifically, the reduction is 86%. Less than one-quarter of the Healthy People 2030 target.

79.7%
Breastfeeding Initiation

Within two hours of birth, compared to significantly lower state and national averages. Immediate skin-to-skin reached 72.9% of MHN births.

263 clients with documented demographic data (Jan 2023 – Dec 2025) 45.1% Medicaid-insured, more than double the state rate 53.6% Black/African American clients IRB #2025-001 · Baton Rouge General Medical Center partnership

Our Name

In the Christian tradition, a teenage Mary travels to the hill country to visit her cousin Elizabeth during Elizabeth's pregnancy. She stays for three months, listening, cooking, walking alongside her, simply being present. The gospel records no advice, no intervention. Only presence.

The Greek word doule (δούλη) means "a woman who serves." That is the first doula in written history, and she is our inspiration. Every volunteer who trains with us is carrying on a two-thousand-year-old practice of accompaniment.

"We doula like the original doula. Continuity, presence, showing up, that is the work."

Founder

Madeline LeBlanc

MHA · RNC-OB · IBCLC · IAT · Founder & Lead Instructor

Born and raised in Baton Rouge, Madeline is a Registered Nurse Certified in Obstetrics, an International Board Certified Lactation Consultant, a birth doula, an educator, and a community advocate with a lifelong passion for supporting mothers and families through some of life's most vulnerable transitions.

Known for her high energy, deep compassion, and contagious passion for birth justice, Madeline is committed to creating a maternal care system that empowers mothers, uplifts families, and centers the whole person: mind, body, and spirit.

She holds advanced degrees in nursing and health administration and actively conducts research on the impact of doula care and peer-based community support on maternal and infant outcomes in hospital birth settings, work that now operates under IRB Protocol #2025-001 in partnership with Baton Rouge General Medical Center.

"Every birth can be a space of dignity, connection, and power. That is what we are building, one pair of hands at a time."Madeline LeBlanc · Founder, Mary's Hands Network
