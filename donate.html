Why It Matters

Louisiana's maternal outcomes are among the worst in the country. We are changing them.

MHN families, most low-income and majority Black, experience outcomes that outperform the national average. These reductions were achieved with trained volunteer doulas, not paid clinicians.

65%
Fewer Cesareans

MHN clients had a 12.6% C-section rate vs. Louisiana's 36.1% baseline. Statistically significant (p<0.001).

84%
Fewer Preterm Births

MHN clients had a 2.1% preterm rate vs. Louisiana's 13.4% baseline. 86% reduction for Black women specifically.

$400M+
Annual Savings if Scaled

Applied to all 60,000 Louisiana births, MHN outcomes would prevent 13,662 cesareans and 6,708 preterm births a year.
